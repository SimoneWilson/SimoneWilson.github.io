The History of Salt Lake City.

The founding of Salt Lake City dates back to 1847, when Brigham Young, a prophet, led a village of pioneers in an effort to escape the violence that plagued the American Midwest at that time. The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is currently headquartered in SLC.

The development and growth of Salt Lake City's economy began with the construction of the 1st Intercontinental Railroad, which gave the region the nickname "The Crossroads of the West". Furthermore, mining activity has always been very strong in the area. Another culminating factor in the development of SLC was the ski-based tourism industry, which brings thousands of people to the region each year.



Demography

Salt Lake City is the capital of Utah, and is the most populous city in the state, with approximately 200,500 inhabitants. According to information from the US government, 78% of the city's population adheres to the Mormon religion. The county is 517 km away from Denver (approximately 8 hours drive), and borders North Salt Lake, South Salt Lake and Woods Cross.

The median household income in Utah was $71,414.00, with South Jordan having the highest value in the state at $101,374.00 and Provo having the lowest value at $48,755.00, probably due to a significant portion of the population of the city being made up of university students. Income inequality in Utah is less extreme than in the country as a whole. Within the state, the most uneven distribution is recorded in Salt Lake City and the lowest in Lehi.



Places to visit in Salt Lake City

If you like historical places, Temple Square is the right place to start. The place has a impressive architecture and you will be able to see the Temple of Salt Lake City and the Mormon Tabernacle. While in downtown, you also can visit the Utah State Capitol, where the Supreme Court and legislative areas are located. The area also has great restaurants and malls.

Salt Lake City has many museums such as Museum of Natural History, The Leonardo and Utah Museum of Fine Arts. Enjoy the tourist attractions, and much more. Although, if you prefer the outdoor life, you need to know the Utah Parks. In the city you can visit Liberty Park and Sugar House Park. Within a few hours drive you can visit: Wasatch-Cache National Forest, Timpanogos Cave, Arches National Park, Zion National Park and others.
