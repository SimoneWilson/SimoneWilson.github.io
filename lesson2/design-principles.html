Design Principles

By Simone McKee

Repetition

The principle of repetition is evident in this website. usps.com This principle simply means the reusing of the same or like elements throughout the design. You can see that the site uses a uniform color scheme, the ux displays the white, the dark blue and the light gray. Also the links are repeatedly placed kind of like a checkerboard making the UI predictable and pleasant.

Hick's Law

It's possible to identify on the website familysearch.org many different design principles, but the principle set by the Hick-Hyman Law is highlighted on this page. The reason that this follows the law is that there is only one choice of button to click. The principle of Hick's law states that the more choices a user has the longer it will take them to make a decision of what to click. By minimizing the number of links that can be clicked on this page, it will minimize the risk of the user getting confused about what to click to get to the next step in the page flow.

White Space and Clear Design

The design principle that this page represents is white space and clean design. The webpage at https://bananarepublic.gap.com follows that principle by having plenty of whitespace in between the different segments of the page and it's a clean design because there is only one image and not too many different colors that all fit with the logo.
